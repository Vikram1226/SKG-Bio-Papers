Environmental Impact

The Disadvantages of
Non-Degradable Plastic Bags

Non-degradable plastic bags have become a part of daily life, but their convenience comes at a major cost to the environment.

Persistent Pollution

Plastic bags take hundreds of years to decompose, leading to accumulation in landfills and natural environments.

Ocean Pollution

Millions of tons of plastic bags end up in oceans, causing severe consequences for marine ecosystems.

Micro plastics Contamination

Over time, plastic bags break down into micro plastics that contaminate soil, water, and food chains.

Resource Depletion

The production of plastic bags requires fossil fuel consumption, contributing to carbon emissions and climate change.

Environmental Impact

The Disadvantages of
Non-Degradable Plastic Bags

Non-degradable plastic bags have become a ubiquitous part of our daily lives, but their convenience comes at a significant cost to the environment.

Persistent Pollution

Plastic bags take hundreds of years to decompose, leading to accumulation in landfills and natural environments, posing a severe threat to wildlife.

Ocean Pollution

Millions of tons of plastic bags end up in our oceans each year, causing devastating consequences for marine life who mistake plastic for food.

Micro plastics Contamination

Over time, plastic bags break down into micro plastics, which contaminate soil and water sources, entering the food chain and harming human health.

Resource Depletion

The production of plastic bags requires fossil fuel consumption, contributing to carbon emissions and exacerbating climate change globally.

SKG Bio Papers is an authorised dealer of MEND-TECH, a CPCB certified company (Certificate No. CPCB-UPC-II/MENDTECH/MADHYAPRADESH/287), duly certified by CIEPT test report vide CIPET:IPT/CHN/PTC/Bio-D./IR/MT/2022-23/22102054.

The ideal replacement for conventional plastic — which does not decline even in 300 years — is compostable bags produced from cornstarch and plant-based materials that are 100% compostable in accordance with IS/ISO 17088.

These bags can be used by shops, retailers, showrooms, industries, hotels, and homes — highly convenient for daily tasks and waste management. By manufacturing and providing compostable bags, we have taken a stand against plastic waste.
